Trauma therapy for high-functioning adults

Many of the adults I work with are capable, thoughtful people who are used to managing a lot on their own. On the outside, life may look successful — but internally it can feel overwhelming.

You might notice:

  • chronic stress or anxiety that won’t turn off
  • feeling emotionally overwhelmed or burned out
  • difficulty relaxing even when things are going well
  • unresolved trauma or painful past experiences
  • patterns in relationships or parenting that keep repeating
  • feeling disconnected from yourself or others

These experiences are often connected to how trauma and chronic stress affect the nervous system. Trauma therapy can help your body and mind process what has been carried for too long so that you can begin to feel more stable, regulated, and present in your life.

What healing from trauma can look like

  • feeling calmer and less reactive to stress
  • being able to relax without feeling on edge
  • experiencing greater emotional balance
  • feeling more present in relationships and parenting
  • less intrusive memories or emotional triggers
  • a stronger sense of internal stability and safety

Healing often happens gradually, but once the nervous system settles, life can feel more manageable and connected.

Virtual EMDR therapy in California

I provide virtual EMDR therapy for adults throughout California, allowing you to access trauma therapy from anywhere in the state. EMDR (Eye Movement Desensitization and Reprocessing) is a research-supported trauma therapy that helps the brain process distressing experiences so they no longer feel overwhelming.

EMDR can be helpful for:

  • trauma and unresolved past experiences
  • anxiety and chronic stress
  • emotional overwhelm
  • burnout in professionals and parents
  • feeling stuck despite insight or self-awareness

Sessions are conducted through secure telehealth, allowing you to access therapy from the privacy of your own home.

About bilateral stimulation (BLS)

Bilateral stimulation (BLS) is a therapeutic technique involving rhythmic, alternating stimulation of the brain's left and right hemispheres, primarily used in EMDR therapy to process trauma and reduce stress.

It engages the brain through visual (eye movements), auditory (tones), or tactile (tapping) methods. Sessions are conducted via secure telehealth for adults located in California.
